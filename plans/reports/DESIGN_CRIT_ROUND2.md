## Verdict: SHIP or ITERATE (one line + 2-sentence justification)

**ITERATE.** The redesign cleared 12/16 slop-watchlist items and turned the verdict card from the worst screen into the best, but it re-fails the one item round 1 explicitly declared a shipping blocker (tab-bar clipping interactive content on Verdict and Profile) and introduces a new worst-pixel contrast failure (1.9:1 subtitle on the amber SRM header). Both judgments independently flagged the same blockers, so this cannot go to a phone as-is — but the punch list is short and surgical, not another redesign.

## Punch list

1. **[Verdict + Profile] Tab-bar clipping — SHIPPING BLOCKER, second consecutive failure.** "Save for Later" is bisected mid-glyph by the floating bar (and anything below it is unreachable); Profile Recent Scans rows 2–3 ghost behind the bar with no scrim. Fix at the container, not per-screen: add a shared `.safeAreaInset(edge: .bottom)` / ~100pt `contentMargins(.bottom)` contract in `MainTabView` (or a `ScrollView` bottom-padding modifier in `DesignSystem.swift`) so `VerdictCardView` and `ProfileTabView` inherit it, plus a bottom fade scrim behind the bar.
2. **[Verdict] Gray subtitle on amber SRM header at 1.92:1** ("Pale Ale · 5.6% ABV" in `~#8E8E93` on `#E7A2xx`). In `VerdictCardView`, stop using the global `textSecondary` token on SRM surfaces — switch the header card's ink to a dark-on-light pair (e.g. `#3A2A10`-family at ~70% opacity), same treatment as the fixed YOUR CALL chip.
3. **[Journal, Detail ×2, Profile] Stout SRM swatch invisible** — near-black fill on `#1A1A1E` reads as a failed image load, and the Profile Stout bar visually reads 0% while printing 33%. In the SRM swatch component / `styleSRMColor()` in `DesignSystem.swift`, add a 1px `Color.white.opacity(0.15)` hairline stroke to all swatches and clamp fill luminance to a floor for dark styles.
4. **[Detail sheets ×2] Save is an orphaned bare gray word** floating at the sheet's bottom edge, ~150px below Delete, indistinguishable from placeholder text and worse-discovered than the baseline's header pill. In `JournalEntryDetailView`, either restore Save as the top-right header pill or give the bottom action real filled-button chrome with a visibly distinct enabled/disabled state — and put Delete *below* it.
5. **[Journal] "Not For Me" chip: 3.0:1 text contrast (regressed from 3.3:1) and wraps to two lines**, inflating the Bud Light row and colliding with the date column. In the shared verdict-chip component, lighten the red text token to clear 4.5:1 on its `#392425` fill and add `.lineLimit(1)` + `.fixedSize(horizontal: true, vertical: false)`.
6. **[Verdict] Hard dark-to-amber seam at y≈40** — the atmosphere starts abruptly at the status-bar boundary instead of running full-height. In `VerdictCardView`, extend the tint gradient behind the status bar with `.ignoresSafeArea(edges: .top)` (one judgment saw this done, the other saw a seam — verify on device, the seam render wins for now).
7. **[Verdict] History pill copy reads truncated:** "You've had this one — you rated it like" — the emoji was swapped for the literal word "like". In `VerdictCardView`, rewrite to a full sentence, e.g. "You've had this one — you gave it a thumbs up."
8. **[Enter Beer] ~340px of dead black between field and CTA where live BeerMatcher suggestions were specified** (crit #6, explicitly asked for, not delivered), and the sheet body is still canvas `#1A1A1E` rather than the elevated `#2A2A2E` token. In the enter-beer sheet view, wire `BeerMatcher` prefix matches into a suggestion list in that zone and move the sheet background to the elevated token.
9. **[Detail sheets ×2] Loop-closer line missing** — crit #14's "We said TRY IT — you gave it ★★★★" comparison was not added on either render. Add the verdict-vs-rating line to `JournalEntryDetailView` when both a stored verdict and a rating exist.
10. **[Check tab] Scan CTA glow reads as an overexposure band** — the halo extends ~20–25px past the full-width button and hits both screen edges; and the do-not-lose copy "Snap a label. We'll tell you if it's worth your money." was replaced with feature-speak. In `CheckTabView`, shrink shadow radius/opacity (or inset the button so the glow never touches screen edges) and restore the original tagline.
